Story of Tribute

In an age long past, when the digital realm of the blockchain was still a vast, uncharted expanse, there existed two companions, Jack and Kyle. These were not ordinary wanderers; they were developers, architects of code, etching their vision into the indelible fabric of the blockchain. Their quest was nothing short of monumental - to birth the most exemplary coin the world had ever seen.

On an eve of destiny, under the spectral glow of countless lines of code, they toiled in a marathon of creation. The room, bathed in the harsh, electric blue light, was a crucible where something extraordinary was taking shape. Abruptly, from the tangled labyrinth of their screens, a figure began to emerge. It was a luminous, holographic manifestation of the elusive entity known only as Satoshi Nakamoto.

The figure spoke in cryptic riddles and complex equations, guiding their hands and minds to the climax of their creation. In the chilling silence that followed, the glow from their screens cast long shadows across their awestruck faces. They had done it. They had created the best coin the world had ever seen.

However, if you dare to ask Jack and Kyle to recount the tale of the best coin in the world, they might falter. The exact specifics have faded with time, blurred by the excitement of invention and the exhaustion of their endeavor. What was once considered the best coin in the world now existed as a hazy memory, its details elusive and faint.

Yet, they will assure you of one thing: while it may no longer hold the title of the best coin in the world, it remains a powerful tribute. A tribute to the limitless potential of the blockchain, a homage to the power of human ingenuity. A tribute to their relentless quest and the extraordinary journey they had embarked on.

And that coin, that gleaming testament to their quest, was merely the opening chapter of their grand adventure. An adventure that, even now, is only just beginning.

In the wake of the coin's creation, its allure stretched across the digital landscape, luring in all who dared venture into the realm of the blockchain. Yet, this was no ordinary coin. It was a masterpiece of code, a testament to human ingenuity, and it bore a unique, enigmatic trait: a curse known as "The Curse of the Weak Hands".

This curse was an intricate part of the coin's design, imprinted deep within its indelible blockchain fabric. Whenever the coin changed hands, a fee was extracted, a tribute to the coin's inherent value and a reminder of its creators' original intent: to encourage long-term belief in their creation.

But there was a way to lift the curse. If a holder possessed the coin for exactly 42 hours, a seemingly arbitrary duration yet imbued with a veiled significance, the curse would be broken, and they could then pass on the coin without the customary fee.

However, should a holder succumb to the lure of immediate profit and relinquish the coin before the designated time, the curse would renew itself. The countdown would reset, and the 42-hour wait would begin anew for the new holder.

In a fascinating twist, the curse had a finite lifespan. Once only 21 billion coins remained, the curse would be permanently lifted. This feature was a testament to the coin's ultimate scarcity and the balance between creation and preservation.

As Jack and Kyle watched their creation make its way through the intricate networks of the digital world, they knew their adventure was far from over. They had merely set the stage for a grand saga in the annals of the blockchain, a saga that was only just beginning.

In the depths of an ancient chamber, the adventurers' torches revealed a sight like no other. Amidst timeworn treasures, an age-old machine rested, enshrining a sack of Tribute, the legendary coin of tales untold.

This mystical mechanism was more than a relic. It bore cryptic inscriptions hinting at its secret. "He who holds a luminous token of the ethereal kind may access the bounty within." The adventurers understood that a shiny coin was needed, but not just any coin. The Tribute was locked away, requiring a token that shimmered with an otherworldly quality.

The adventurers, though weary, felt a surge of determination. They understood the challenge posed by the ancient device. The Tribute was within their grasp, but they needed to procure an exceptional token.

The echo of their steps resonated in the ancient chamber as they resumed their quest, a single thought echoing in their minds - the Tribute was waiting, and the key to it shimmered somewhere in the shadows of the unknown.

Frequently Asked Questions

Question: Is it safe to use Tribute token?
Answer: The Tribute token is designed with security in mind. The token contract has been carefully audited and does not have any mint or abusable functions. The only function available is the Whitelist, which is used to add current and future liquidity pools. This ensures that buyers are not affected by diminishing tax fees. Additionally, the Whitelist function allows us to add exchange wallets, ensuring that tax rules do not impact their withdrawals and transactions, allowing us to easily comply with their regulations.
Question: How can I claim my Tribute tokens?
Answer: To claim Tribute tokens, you need to have at least 0.1 ETH in your wallet. If you hold 10 or more ETH, you will be able to claim the maximum amount of tokens claimable for an address. Additionally, a limited allocation of 7% of the total supply is being airdropped to ETH holders. Please note that the airdrop is limited to the total allocated amount and will be distributed on a first-come, first-served basis. Each address can only claim the airdrop once. Make sure to have sufficient gas fees and use a compatible wallet that supports the Tribute token
Question: What is the total supply of Tribute tokens?
Answer: The total supply of Tribute tokens is 42 billion, inspired by the famous phrase "The answer to life, the universe, and everything." In a playful homage to this concept, the token supply has been set to 42 billion. The Tribute ecosystem implements a deflationary mechanism through taxes. If non-burned (dead address) tokens reach 21 billion, which is half of the initial supply, the tax is completely removed. This means that the tax feature creates deflationary pressure until only 21 billion Tribute tokens are left in circulation. This design ensures the scarcity and value preservation of the remaining tokens.
Question: How does the tax mechanism work?
Answer: Whenever someone sends or receives Tribute tokens, a diminishing tax rate comes into effect. It starts with a total tax fee of 10% and gradually decreases to 0% if you can hold the tokens for 42 hours. This mechanism, known as the "Curse of the Weak Hands," discourages short-term trading and promotes long-term holding. It also helps combat bots, as they would be subject to the maximum tax rate if they attempt to manipulate transactions. If you don't send or receive Tribute tokens for 42 hours, you can make a transaction with no fee applied. It's important to note that every send or receive of Tribute tokens resets this 42-hour timer.
Question: How can we be included in the whitelist to not be affected by the tax?
Answer: If you are an exchange or a liquidity pool and would like to be included in the whitelist to avoid being affected by the tax, please use one of the available options to contact the developer. Please note that the whitelist is specifically designed for liquidity pools or exchanges. As the community grows, the management of the whitelist command will be handed over to a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) with a fair voting mechanism.
